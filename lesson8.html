Lesson 8: Ethics in Computing

1. Responsibility

Establishing the three pillars of responsible technology use is essential for every computing professional.

  • Legal Issues: Focuses on strict compliance with government regulations and data laws.
  • Ethics: Acts as a moral compass to determine if an action is "right" or "fair," even if not explicitly forbidden by law.
  • Societal Issues: Examines how technological choices impact the well-being of the community and the evolution of culture.
2. Ownership

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) protect the creations of the mind in the digital space.

  • Copyright: Provides creators with the exclusive legal right to manage, publish, and sell their work.
  • Plagiarism: The unauthorized replication of ideas and language; strictly prohibited in academic and professional fields.
  • Case Study: Real-world legal battles, such as Silicon Knights v. Epic Games, serve as reminders to respect software licensing.
3. Misconduct

Digital environments face various forms of misuse that can harm individuals and industries.

  • Cyberbullying: Using social platforms for humiliation or harassment.
  • Phishing: Deceptive practices used to steal sensitive financial or personal data.
  • Digital Piracy: Robs creators of revenue through the unlawful duplication of digital goods.
  • Computer Addiction: Highlights the personal cost of excessive technology use on daily human interaction.
4. Legislation

In the Philippines, specific laws ensure system integrity and protect users.

  • R.A. 10175 (Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012): The primary legal defense against digital threats.
  • Criminalized Acts: Specifically targets Illegal Access (hacking), Data Interference (unauthorized alteration), and Online Libel.
  • Consequences: Ensures there are real-world legal penalties for digital actions that harm others.
References: COMP001 Module 07: Legal, Ethical, and Societal Issues; Republic Act No. 10175 (Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012).
